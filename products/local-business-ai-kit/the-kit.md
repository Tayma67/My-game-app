# The Local Business AI Kit — More Reviews, Bookings & Repeat Customers

12 copy-paste AI prompts for restaurants, salons, shops, and local services to
get more reviews, fill quiet days, and keep customers coming back — on a free
ChatGPT or Claude account. No marketing budget required.

> How to use: replace [BRACKETS]. Keep claims truthful and follow platform
> rules (e.g., never offer payment for reviews). AI drafts; you approve.

---

## Part 1 — Get Found & Get Reviews

## 1. Optimize your Google Business description
> Write a Google Business Profile description for "[BUSINESS TYPE]" in
> "[AREA]". Include what we do, what makes us local/special, and natural
> keywords people search. Friendly, ≤750 characters.

## 2. Ask for reviews the right way
> Write 3 short, warm messages asking a happy customer for a Google review
> (SMS, email, in-person card). Make it 1-tap easy, never pushy, never offer
> incentives. Include a line they can copy if unsure what to write.

## 3. Reply to reviews (good and bad)
> Write replies to these reviews for "[BUSINESS]": a 5-star one (warm, specific
> thanks) and a 2-star one "[PASTE]" (calm, takes responsibility, offers to
> make it right offline). Protect our reputation.

## Part 2 — Fill Quiet Days

## 4. Promotion ideas for slow periods
> Suggest 5 low-cost promotions to fill quiet [days/times] for a "[BUSINESS
> TYPE]" in "[AREA]". For each: the offer, who it targets, and a 1-line pitch.
> Keep margins healthy.

## 5. SMS/WhatsApp blast to past customers
> Write a short SMS to past customers announcing "[OFFER]". Friendly, ≤160
> characters, clear value, one CTA (book/visit), easy opt-out line.

## 6. Local social post
> Write an Instagram/Facebook post for "[BUSINESS]" promoting "[OFFER/EVENT]".
> Local and warm, a strong photo idea, 5 local hashtags, clear CTA.

---

## Part 3 — Keep Customers Coming Back

## 7. Simple loyalty/repeat offer
> Design a simple repeat-customer offer for "[BUSINESS TYPE]" (punch card,
> "next visit" discount, or VIP list). Explain how it works and a message to
> introduce it to customers.

## 8. Win-back message for lapsed customers
> Write a warm "we miss you" message to customers who haven't visited in a
> while, with a small reason to return. Two versions: SMS and email.

## 9. Birthday / occasion message
> Write a friendly birthday/occasion message offering a small treat at
> "[BUSINESS]". Personal, low-pressure, makes them feel valued.

---

## Part 4 — Run It Smoother

## 10. Answer common customer questions
> You are front-desk for "[BUSINESS]". Our info: [hours, services, prices,
> location]. Customer asks: "[PASTE]". Answer in ≤80 words using only these
> facts; if unknown, offer to check.

## 11. Menu / service descriptions
> Write appetizing, accurate descriptions for these [menu items/services]:
> "[LIST]". Each ≤30 words, sensory and clear, no over-claiming.

## 12. Staff shout-out / hiring post
> Write a short post to [celebrate a team member / announce we're hiring] at
> "[BUSINESS]". Warm, on-brand, with a clear next step.
