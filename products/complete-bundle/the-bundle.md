# AI Automation Starter Kit — The 10 Blueprints

A practical, no-code kit. Each blueprint = the problem it solves, the free
tool stack, the steps, and the exact AI prompt to paste. Built to run on a
free ChatGPT/Claude account plus free tiers of common tools.

> How to use: pick ONE blueprint that hurts most this week. Set it up. Only
> then add a second. Trying all 10 at once is the #1 reason people quit.

---

## Recommended free/low-cost stack
- **AI model:** ChatGPT or Claude (free tier is enough to start)
- **Connector (optional):** Make.com or n8n free tier — only if you want
  hands-off triggering. Manual copy-paste works for all 10 to start.
- **Email/Docs:** Gmail + Google Docs/Sheets (free)

---

## 1. Instant lead reply drafts
**Problem:** Slow replies lose leads. **Stack:** AI + email.
**Steps:** Paste the incoming message into the prompt → get 2 reply options →
edit → send.
**Prompt:**
> You are my sales assistant. Here is an inbound message from a potential
> client: "[PASTE]". Write two short, warm reply drafts (max 90 words each):
> one friendly-casual, one professional. End each with a single clear next
> step (a question or a booking link). No fluff.

## 2. Content repurposing engine
**Problem:** One idea, many platforms, no time.
**Prompt:**
> Turn this into a content pack: "[PASTE blog/idea/transcript]". Output:
> (a) 1 LinkedIn post, (b) 3 tweets, (c) 1 Instagram caption with 5
> hashtags, (d) 1 short-video script (≤45s) with a hook in the first line.
> Keep my voice plain and concrete. No emojis unless natural.

## 3. Invoice / payment reminder writer
**Prompt:**
> Write a polite payment reminder for an overdue invoice. Client: [NAME].
> Invoice: [#], amount [AMOUNT], due [DATE], now [X days] late. Tone: firm
> but friendly, assume good faith, give an easy way to pay. 3 escalating
> versions: gentle, direct, final-notice.

## 4. Customer FAQ / support responder
**Prompt:**
> You are support for [BUSINESS, one line]. Our policies: [PASTE 3-6 bullet
> facts]. Customer asks: "[PASTE]". Answer in ≤120 words, accurate to the
> facts only. If the facts don't cover it, say you'll check and ask one
> clarifying question. Never invent policy.

## 5. Weekly business report from raw notes
**Prompt:**
> Here are my rough notes/numbers for the week: "[PASTE]". Produce a 1-page
> report: Wins, Problems, Numbers (as a small table), Next week's top 3
> priorities. Be concise and honest; flag anything that looks off.

## 6. Social post batch (a week in one go)
**Prompt:**
> Generate 7 short social posts for [BUSINESS/niche]. Mix: 3 value/tips,
> 2 behind-the-scenes, 1 soft promo, 1 question to the audience. Each ≤280
> chars, distinct hook, no hashtag spam. Number them Mon–Sun.

## 7. Cold-email first-draft (personalized)
**Prompt:**
> Write a cold email to [PROSPECT/role] at [COMPANY]. What I offer: [ONE
> line]. One specific, true observation about them: "[PASTE]". Rules: ≤120
> words, no flattery, lead with their problem, one soft CTA. Subject line
> ≤6 words.
> (Reality check: generic cold email gets ~2-5% replies. Personalize the
> observation line by hand — that's where replies come from.)

## 8. Meeting notes → action items
**Prompt:**
> Here is a meeting transcript/notes: "[PASTE]". Output: (a) 3-sentence
> summary, (b) decisions made, (c) action items as "Owner — task — due",
> (d) open questions. Don't invent owners or dates not in the text.

## 9. Review / testimonial request
**Prompt:**
> Write a short message asking a happy customer for a review. Customer:
> [NAME], bought [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Tone: grateful, low-pressure, make it
> 1-click easy. Give 2 versions: email and WhatsApp/DM. Include a one-line
> prompt the customer can copy if they're stuck on what to write.

## 10. Spreadsheet / data cleanup helper
**Prompt:**
> I'll paste messy data. Task: [normalize names / split full name into
> first+last / fix date formats to YYYY-MM-DD / dedupe]. Return a clean
> table only, same row count unless deduping (then tell me how many you
> removed). Data: "[PASTE]".

---

## Going hands-off (optional, later)
Once a blueprint earns its keep manually, wire it with Make.com or n8n
(free tier): trigger (new email / form / schedule) → AI step (paste the
prompt as the system message) → action (send email / append to Sheet).
Start manual. Automate only what you've proven you'll use.

# Freelancer Win-the-Client Pack — Proposals, DMs & Pricing Scripts

Win more clients without sounding desperate. Copy-paste templates for the
moments that actually decide whether you get hired and paid well.

> How to use: replace [BRACKETS], keep it short, send. Confidence comes from
> clarity, not length.

---

## 1. Upwork/Fiverr proposal (the 5-line formula)
> Hi [NAME] — you need [restate their goal in their words]. I've done this
> before: [1 specific result/example]. Here's how I'd approach yours:
> [step 1], [step 2], [step 3]. I can start [when] and deliver a first
> version by [date]. One question: [smart question that shows you read the
> brief]?

## 2. "No portfolio yet" opener (turns weakness into offer)
> I'm building my portfolio in [niche], so I'll do this at a focused rate
> in exchange for a testimonial if you're happy. You get [clear deliverable]
> with unlimited revisions until it's right. Want me to send a quick plan?

## 3. Price-anchoring reply (when they ask "how much?")
> Projects like this usually run [$X–$Y] depending on [1-2 scope factors].
> For your scope I'd suggest [$Z], fixed, so there are no surprises.
> Want me to break down exactly what's included?

## 4. Handling "that's too expensive"
> Totally fair. Two options: I can reduce scope to [smaller deliverable] for
> [$lower], or keep full scope and split into 2 payments. Which works better?
> (Don't just drop the price for the same work — change the scope.)

## 5. The "scope creep" boundary (kind but firm)
> Happy to add [new request] — it's outside our current scope, so it'd be
> [+$X / +Y days]. Want me to fold it in? Just confirm and I'll proceed.

## 6. Asking for the deposit (no awkwardness)
> Great — to lock in your slot I take 50% upfront, balance on delivery.
> I'll send the invoice/link now; I start as soon as it's in. Sound good?

## 7. Delivery message that earns the next job
> Here's [deliverable]. Quick notes: [1-2 things to know]. If anything needs
> tweaking, tell me and I'll fix it. If you're happy, a short review really
> helps me — and I'd love to be your go-to for [related service].

## 8. Re-engaging a quiet lead (the gentle nudge)
> Hi [NAME] — still keen to help with [project]. If timing's off, no worries;
> if you want to move, I have a slot opening [when]. Want it?

## 9. Raising your rates (for repeat clients)
> Quick heads-up: from [date] my rate for new projects is [$X]. Your ongoing
> work stays at the current rate through [date]. Thanks for being a great
> client to work with.

## 10. Turning one project into a retainer
> Instead of one-off requests, I can keep [outcome] running for you at
> [$X/month] — [what's included]. Predictable for you, no re-quoting each
> time. Want to try it for a month?

# The Job Seeker's AI Kit — Land Interviews Faster

12 copy-paste AI prompts that turn a blank page into a tailored resume,
cover letter, LinkedIn profile, and interview answers — in minutes, using a
free ChatGPT or Claude account.

> How to use: replace [BRACKETS] with your details. Always read the output and
> make it true to you — AI drafts fast, but you are the final editor. Never
> send anything you can't stand behind in an interview.

---

## Part 1 — Resume

## 1. Tailor your resume to a specific job
> Act as a professional resume writer. Here is my current resume: "[PASTE]".
> Here is the job description: "[PASTE]". Rewrite my resume bullet points to
> match this role: mirror the job's keywords, lead each bullet with a strong
> action verb, and quantify impact where possible. Keep everything truthful —
> do not invent experience. Output the revised bullets grouped by job.

## 2. Turn duties into achievements
> Rewrite these job duties as achievement-focused bullets using the format
> "Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y] by doing [Z]". If a metric is missing,
> show the bullet and ask me one short question to get the number. Duties:
> "[PASTE]".

## 3. Beat the ATS (keyword check)
> Compare my resume against this job description and list: (a) important
> keywords/skills in the job that are MISSING from my resume, (b) where I
> could naturally add each, (c) any that I should NOT add because I lack the
> skill. Resume: "[PASTE]". Job: "[PASTE]".

## 4. Write a punchy professional summary
> Write 3 versions of a 2-3 sentence resume summary for a [ROLE] with [X
> years] experience in [field], strongest in [skills]. Tones: confident,
> understated, results-driven. No clichés like "hard-working team player".

---

## Part 2 — Cover Letter

## 5. Draft a tailored cover letter
> Write a cover letter for [ROLE] at [COMPANY]. Use my background: "[PASTE
> resume or notes]". Job: "[PASTE]". Rules: 3 short paragraphs, open with why
> THIS company (reference something specific), middle = 2 relevant proofs,
> close with a confident CTA. No "To whom it may concern", no fluff.

## 6. The "why this company" paragraph
> Here's what the company says about itself: "[PASTE from their site/about
> page]". Write one specific, genuine paragraph on why I want to work there —
> concrete, not flattery. Avoid generic lines anyone could write.

---

## Part 3 — LinkedIn

## 7. Rewrite your LinkedIn headline
> Write 5 LinkedIn headline options for a [ROLE] focused on [specialty].
> Each ≤220 characters, keyword-rich for recruiter search, not just a job
> title. Mix outcome-focused and skill-focused.

## 8. Rewrite your LinkedIn About section
> Write a first-person LinkedIn "About" section for a [ROLE]. Background:
> "[PASTE]". 3 short paragraphs: hook, what I do + proof, what I'm looking for
> / how to reach me. Warm but professional, no buzzword soup.

## 9. Recruiter outreach message
> Write a short LinkedIn message to a recruiter for [ROLE] at [COMPANY].
> ≤80 words, polite, specific about fit, one clear ask (a quick chat). Give a
> casual and a formal version.

---

## Part 4 — Interview

## 10. Predict the interview questions
> Based on this job description, list the 10 most likely interview questions,
> grouped into behavioral, technical, and role-specific. For each, add one
> line on what the interviewer is really testing. Job: "[PASTE]".

## 11. Build STAR answers
> Help me answer "[QUESTION]" using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action,
> Result). Here's my raw experience: "[PASTE]". Keep it ≤90 seconds spoken,
> end on a quantified result, and flag anything that sounds rehearsed.

## 12. Smart questions to ask them
> Give me 6 thoughtful questions to ask at the end of an interview for [ROLE]
> at [COMPANY] that show I researched them and care about impact — not
> salary/vacation. Base them on: "[PASTE about the company/role]".

---

## Bonus: the 1-day job-application workflow
1. Prompt #3 → fix keyword gaps. 2. Prompt #1 → tailor resume.
3. Prompt #5 → cover letter. 4. Prompt #10 + #11 → prep answers.
Repeat per application. With these, a tailored application takes ~20 minutes
instead of 2 hours.
