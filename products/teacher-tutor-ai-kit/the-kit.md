# The Teacher & Tutor's AI Kit — Plan Lessons & Save Hours

12 copy-paste AI prompts to plan lessons, create materials, communicate with
parents, and grade faster — on a free ChatGPT or Claude account. For teachers,
tutors, and homeschool parents.

> How to use: replace [BRACKETS]. Always review AI output for accuracy and
> age-appropriateness before using with students. You are the educator; AI is
> a drafting assistant.

---

## Part 1 — Plan

## 1. Build a lesson plan
> Act as an experienced [subject] teacher. Create a [45-minute] lesson plan on
> "[TOPIC]" for [grade/level]: objective, hook, direct instruction, guided
> practice, independent practice, and a quick assessment. Include timing.

## 2. Differentiate for all learners
> Take this lesson on "[TOPIC]" and give me three versions: one for students
> who need support, one on-level, and one extension/challenge. Same objective,
> adjusted scaffolding.

## 3. Unit / week overview
> Outline a [1-week] unit on "[TOPIC]" for [level]: daily objectives, key
> vocabulary, activities, and an end-of-unit assessment idea.

---

## Part 2 — Create Materials

## 4. Quiz / worksheet generator
> Create a [10-question] quiz on "[TOPIC]" for [level]: a mix of multiple
> choice, short answer, and one applied question. Include an answer key.

## 5. Explain a concept simply
> Explain "[CONCEPT]" to a [grade] student in simple terms, with an everyday
> analogy and one quick check-for-understanding question.

## 6. Discussion questions & activities
> Give me 8 engaging discussion questions and 3 hands-on activity ideas for
> "[TOPIC]" at [level] that encourage critical thinking.

---

## Part 3 — Communicate

## 7. Parent update / newsletter
> Write a warm class newsletter for [week/month]: what we learned, what's
> coming, and one way parents can help at home. Friendly, concise.

## 8. Difficult parent message
> Help me write a professional, empathetic message to a parent about "[ISSUE,
> e.g., missing work / behavior]". Factual, solution-focused, partnership tone.

## 9. Student feedback that motivates
> Write encouraging, specific feedback for a student on "[WORK/SITUATION]":
> name a strength, one clear next step, and a motivating close. Growth-minded.

---

## Part 4 — Save Time

## 10. Grade short answers faster
> Here is a rubric "[PASTE]" and a student response "[PASTE]". Give a score
> with 2 lines of specific, kind feedback tied to the rubric. (I review before
> finalizing.)

## 11. Sub plans in minutes
> Write clear emergency sub plans for [subject], [level], for one day: a simple
> lesson a substitute can run, materials needed, and classroom-management notes.

## 12. Report-card comments
> Write 3 report-card comment options for a student who is "[brief description
> of performance]": specific, professional, balanced (strength + growth area).
