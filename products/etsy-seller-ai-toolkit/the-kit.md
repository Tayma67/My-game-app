# The Etsy Seller's AI Toolkit — Listings That Get Found & Sold

11 copy-paste AI prompts to write SEO listings, find tags, answer buyers, and
plan promotions for your Etsy shop — using a free ChatGPT or Claude account.
No marketing degree, no paid tools.

> How to use: replace [BRACKETS] with your product details. AI gives you a
> strong first draft fast; tweak it to sound like your shop. Always check
> Etsy's current policies before using any claim.

---

## Part 1 — Get Found (Etsy SEO)

## 1. Write a keyword-rich listing title
> Act as an Etsy SEO expert. My product: "[DESCRIBE]". Write 5 listing-title
> options under 140 characters. Front-load the most-searched keywords a buyer
> would type, stay readable (not keyword spam), and avoid words Etsy ignores.

## 2. Generate 13 Etsy tags
> Give me 13 Etsy tags (max 20 characters each) for this product: "[DESCRIBE]".
> Mix broad and long-tail buyer search phrases, include occasion/style/recipient
> variations, no single-word tags wasted, no repeats of the exact title.

## 3. Write the listing description
> Write an Etsy listing description for "[PRODUCT]". Structure: (1) a 2-line
> hook on the benefit/feeling, (2) bullet specs (size, material, options),
> (3) who it's perfect for / occasions, (4) shipping & care note, (5) a soft
> CTA to favorite or message. Warm, scannable, benefit-first.

## 4. Find new keyword angles
> Brainstorm 15 buyer search phrases for "[PRODUCT]" beyond the obvious —
> include gift occasions, recipient types, aesthetics/trends, and problems it
> solves. Mark which are likely high-competition vs. long-tail.

---

## Part 2 — Convert Browsers to Buyers

## 5. Variations & upsell ideas
> Suggest 5 product variations or add-ons I could offer for "[PRODUCT]" to
> raise average order value (e.g., personalization, bundles, sizes, gift
> wrap). For each, a 1-line reason a buyer would want it.

## 6. Pricing sanity check
> Help me price "[PRODUCT]". My costs: materials [X], time [Y hrs], target
> hourly [Z]. Etsy fees ~6.5% + payment + listing. Show a cost breakdown and
> suggest a price with healthy margin, plus a "premium/gift" tier.

## 7. Photo & flat-lay shot list
> Give me a 7-shot photo checklist to sell "[PRODUCT]" on Etsy: which angles,
> a scale/context shot, a lifestyle shot idea, and what each photo should make
> the buyer feel. Free-phone-camera friendly.

---

## Part 3 — Buyer Communication

## 8. Friendly buyer message templates
> Write short, warm Etsy message templates for: (a) order received/thank you,
> (b) shipped + tracking, (c) a delay apology, (d) asking for a review after
> delivery. Each ≤60 words, on-brand for a small handmade shop.

## 9. Handle a negative-review situation
> A buyer is unhappy: "[PASTE their message]". Write a calm, professional
> reply that takes responsibility where fair, offers a concrete fix
> (replacement/refund/partial), and protects my shop's reputation. Give 2
> tones: warm and firm-but-kind.

---

## Part 4 — Grow

## 10. A month of social posts to drive traffic
> Write 12 short social captions to promote my Etsy shop "[SHOP/NICHE]" over a
> month: mix product highlights, behind-the-scenes, customer love, and a soft
> sale. Each ≤200 chars with a clear hook. No hashtag spam.

## 11. Seasonal promotion planner
> Suggest a 3-month promotion calendar for an Etsy shop selling "[NICHE]":
> which holidays/occasions to target, a sale or bundle idea for each, and when
> to update titles/tags for the season.

---

## Bonus: new-listing workflow (10 minutes)
Prompt #1 (title) → #2 (tags) → #3 (description) → #7 (photos) → publish.
Then #8 keeps buyers happy and #4/#10 bring traffic.
