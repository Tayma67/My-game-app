# The Fitness Coach's AI Kit — Sign Clients, Write Programs, Keep Them

12 copy-paste AI prompts to attract clients, write programs and check-ins, and
keep members motivated — on a free ChatGPT or Claude account. For personal
trainers, online coaches, and gym owners.

> How to use: replace [BRACKETS]. AI drafts the words; you provide the coaching
> expertise. Never give medical advice — refer clients to professionals when
> health issues arise.

---

## Part 1 — Attract Clients

## 1. Niche positioning statement
> I'm a [type] coach helping "[WHO]" achieve "[GOAL]". Write 5 clear, specific
> positioning statements (no "transform your life" clichés) that speak to a
> real outcome my ideal client wants.

## 2. Content ideas that attract clients
> Give me 20 short-form content ideas for a "[NICHE]" fitness coach that build
> trust and attract clients: myths, quick tips, client wins, day-in-the-life.
> Each with a hook line.

## 3. Free lead-magnet outline
> Outline a simple free resource (e.g., "5-day kickstart" or "grocery list")
> that my ideal client "[DESCRIBE]" would trade their email for. Title + 5
> sections + the one result it promises.

---

## Part 2 — Convert

## 4. Discovery / consult call guide
> Write a consult-call framework for a "[SERVICE]": warm opening, 8 questions
> uncovering the client's goal, history, and obstacles, a transition to my
> offer, and a soft close. Supportive, not salesy.

## 5. Handle "I can't afford it"
> A prospect says they can't afford coaching: "[CONTEXT]". Write a respectful
> reply that reframes value vs. cost, offers a smaller entry option, and keeps
> the door open. No pressure.

## 6. Package & pricing tiers
> Design 3 coaching packages (starter / core / premium) for "[NICHE]". For
> each: who it's for, what's included, and the transformation. Make the core
> the obvious choice.

---

## Part 3 — Deliver

## 7. Workout program description
> Write a clear, motivating description of a [X-week] program for a client at
> "[LEVEL]" with goal "[GOAL]": what it includes, weekly structure, and what
> results to expect. Honest, no overpromising. (I provide the actual exercises.)

## 8. Weekly check-in message
> Write a warm weekly check-in for a client working toward "[GOAL]": celebrate
> a win, ask about adherence/energy/obstacles, and set one focus for the week.
> Supportive accountability.

## 9. Nutrition guidance message (non-medical)
> Write general, non-medical nutrition guidance for a client wanting "[GOAL]":
> simple habits, balanced framing, no extreme claims. Add a line to consult a
> doctor/dietitian for medical needs.

---

## Part 4 — Retain & Grow

## 10. Re-motivate a struggling client
> A client is losing motivation: "[SITUATION]". Write an encouraging message
> that normalizes the dip, refocuses on their why, and gives one tiny next
> step. Empathetic, not preachy.

## 11. Win-back lapsed members
> Write a warm "we miss you" message to a lapsed client/member with a low-
> pressure reason to return. Two versions: text and email.

## 12. Referral & testimonial request
> Write a natural message asking a happy client (result: "[RESULT]") for a
> testimonial and a referral. Specific, low-pressure, easy to say yes to.
