# AI Automation Starter Kit — The 10 Blueprints

A practical, no-code kit. Each blueprint = the problem it solves, the free
tool stack, the steps, and the exact AI prompt to paste. Built to run on a
free ChatGPT/Claude account plus free tiers of common tools.

> How to use: pick ONE blueprint that hurts most this week. Set it up. Only
> then add a second. Trying all 10 at once is the #1 reason people quit.

---

## Recommended free/low-cost stack
- **AI model:** ChatGPT or Claude (free tier is enough to start)
- **Connector (optional):** Make.com or n8n free tier — only if you want
  hands-off triggering. Manual copy-paste works for all 10 to start.
- **Email/Docs:** Gmail + Google Docs/Sheets (free)

---

## 1. Instant lead reply drafts
**Problem:** Slow replies lose leads. **Stack:** AI + email.
**Steps:** Paste the incoming message into the prompt → get 2 reply options →
edit → send.
**Prompt:**
> You are my sales assistant. Here is an inbound message from a potential
> client: "[PASTE]". Write two short, warm reply drafts (max 90 words each):
> one friendly-casual, one professional. End each with a single clear next
> step (a question or a booking link). No fluff.

## 2. Content repurposing engine
**Problem:** One idea, many platforms, no time.
**Prompt:**
> Turn this into a content pack: "[PASTE blog/idea/transcript]". Output:
> (a) 1 LinkedIn post, (b) 3 tweets, (c) 1 Instagram caption with 5
> hashtags, (d) 1 short-video script (≤45s) with a hook in the first line.
> Keep my voice plain and concrete. No emojis unless natural.

## 3. Invoice / payment reminder writer
**Prompt:**
> Write a polite payment reminder for an overdue invoice. Client: [NAME].
> Invoice: [#], amount [AMOUNT], due [DATE], now [X days] late. Tone: firm
> but friendly, assume good faith, give an easy way to pay. 3 escalating
> versions: gentle, direct, final-notice.

## 4. Customer FAQ / support responder
**Prompt:**
> You are support for [BUSINESS, one line]. Our policies: [PASTE 3-6 bullet
> facts]. Customer asks: "[PASTE]". Answer in ≤120 words, accurate to the
> facts only. If the facts don't cover it, say you'll check and ask one
> clarifying question. Never invent policy.

## 5. Weekly business report from raw notes
**Prompt:**
> Here are my rough notes/numbers for the week: "[PASTE]". Produce a 1-page
> report: Wins, Problems, Numbers (as a small table), Next week's top 3
> priorities. Be concise and honest; flag anything that looks off.

## 6. Social post batch (a week in one go)
**Prompt:**
> Generate 7 short social posts for [BUSINESS/niche]. Mix: 3 value/tips,
> 2 behind-the-scenes, 1 soft promo, 1 question to the audience. Each ≤280
> chars, distinct hook, no hashtag spam. Number them Mon–Sun.

## 7. Cold-email first-draft (personalized)
**Prompt:**
> Write a cold email to [PROSPECT/role] at [COMPANY]. What I offer: [ONE
> line]. One specific, true observation about them: "[PASTE]". Rules: ≤120
> words, no flattery, lead with their problem, one soft CTA. Subject line
> ≤6 words.
> (Reality check: generic cold email gets ~2-5% replies. Personalize the
> observation line by hand — that's where replies come from.)

## 8. Meeting notes → action items
**Prompt:**
> Here is a meeting transcript/notes: "[PASTE]". Output: (a) 3-sentence
> summary, (b) decisions made, (c) action items as "Owner — task — due",
> (d) open questions. Don't invent owners or dates not in the text.

## 9. Review / testimonial request
**Prompt:**
> Write a short message asking a happy customer for a review. Customer:
> [NAME], bought [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Tone: grateful, low-pressure, make it
> 1-click easy. Give 2 versions: email and WhatsApp/DM. Include a one-line
> prompt the customer can copy if they're stuck on what to write.

## 10. Spreadsheet / data cleanup helper
**Prompt:**
> I'll paste messy data. Task: [normalize names / split full name into
> first+last / fix date formats to YYYY-MM-DD / dedupe]. Return a clean
> table only, same row count unless deduping (then tell me how many you
> removed). Data: "[PASTE]".

---

## Going hands-off (optional, later)
Once a blueprint earns its keep manually, wire it with Make.com or n8n
(free tier): trigger (new email / form / schedule) → AI step (paste the
prompt as the system message) → action (send email / append to Sheet).
Start manual. Automate only what you've proven you'll use.
