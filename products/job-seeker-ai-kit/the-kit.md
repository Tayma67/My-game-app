# The Job Seeker's AI Kit — Land Interviews Faster

12 copy-paste AI prompts that turn a blank page into a tailored resume,
cover letter, LinkedIn profile, and interview answers — in minutes, using a
free ChatGPT or Claude account.

> How to use: replace [BRACKETS] with your details. Always read the output and
> make it true to you — AI drafts fast, but you are the final editor. Never
> send anything you can't stand behind in an interview.

---

## Part 1 — Resume

## 1. Tailor your resume to a specific job
> Act as a professional resume writer. Here is my current resume: "[PASTE]".
> Here is the job description: "[PASTE]". Rewrite my resume bullet points to
> match this role: mirror the job's keywords, lead each bullet with a strong
> action verb, and quantify impact where possible. Keep everything truthful —
> do not invent experience. Output the revised bullets grouped by job.

## 2. Turn duties into achievements
> Rewrite these job duties as achievement-focused bullets using the format
> "Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y] by doing [Z]". If a metric is missing,
> show the bullet and ask me one short question to get the number. Duties:
> "[PASTE]".

## 3. Beat the ATS (keyword check)
> Compare my resume against this job description and list: (a) important
> keywords/skills in the job that are MISSING from my resume, (b) where I
> could naturally add each, (c) any that I should NOT add because I lack the
> skill. Resume: "[PASTE]". Job: "[PASTE]".

## 4. Write a punchy professional summary
> Write 3 versions of a 2-3 sentence resume summary for a [ROLE] with [X
> years] experience in [field], strongest in [skills]. Tones: confident,
> understated, results-driven. No clichés like "hard-working team player".

---

## Part 2 — Cover Letter

## 5. Draft a tailored cover letter
> Write a cover letter for [ROLE] at [COMPANY]. Use my background: "[PASTE
> resume or notes]". Job: "[PASTE]". Rules: 3 short paragraphs, open with why
> THIS company (reference something specific), middle = 2 relevant proofs,
> close with a confident CTA. No "To whom it may concern", no fluff.

## 6. The "why this company" paragraph
> Here's what the company says about itself: "[PASTE from their site/about
> page]". Write one specific, genuine paragraph on why I want to work there —
> concrete, not flattery. Avoid generic lines anyone could write.

---

## Part 3 — LinkedIn

## 7. Rewrite your LinkedIn headline
> Write 5 LinkedIn headline options for a [ROLE] focused on [specialty].
> Each ≤220 characters, keyword-rich for recruiter search, not just a job
> title. Mix outcome-focused and skill-focused.

## 8. Rewrite your LinkedIn About section
> Write a first-person LinkedIn "About" section for a [ROLE]. Background:
> "[PASTE]". 3 short paragraphs: hook, what I do + proof, what I'm looking for
> / how to reach me. Warm but professional, no buzzword soup.

## 9. Recruiter outreach message
> Write a short LinkedIn message to a recruiter for [ROLE] at [COMPANY].
> ≤80 words, polite, specific about fit, one clear ask (a quick chat). Give a
> casual and a formal version.

---

## Part 4 — Interview

## 10. Predict the interview questions
> Based on this job description, list the 10 most likely interview questions,
> grouped into behavioral, technical, and role-specific. For each, add one
> line on what the interviewer is really testing. Job: "[PASTE]".

## 11. Build STAR answers
> Help me answer "[QUESTION]" using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action,
> Result). Here's my raw experience: "[PASTE]". Keep it ≤90 seconds spoken,
> end on a quantified result, and flag anything that sounds rehearsed.

## 12. Smart questions to ask them
> Give me 6 thoughtful questions to ask at the end of an interview for [ROLE]
> at [COMPANY] that show I researched them and care about impact — not
> salary/vacation. Base them on: "[PASTE about the company/role]".

## Bonus: the 1-day job-application workflow
1. Prompt #3 → fix keyword gaps. 2. Prompt #1 → tailor resume.
3. Prompt #5 → cover letter. 4. Prompt #10 + #11 → prep answers.
Repeat per application. With these, a tailored application takes ~20 minutes
instead of 2 hours.
