# The Real Estate Agent's AI Kit — Listings, Leads & Follow-up

12 copy-paste AI prompts to write listing descriptions, follow up with leads,
and handle client communication — in minutes, on a free ChatGPT or Claude
account. No code, no expensive CRM add-ons.

> How to use: replace [BRACKETS]. Always verify facts (square footage, prices,
> disclosures) and follow your local real estate advertising rules. AI drafts;
> you are responsible for accuracy and compliance.

---

## Part 1 — Listings

## 1. Write a listing description
> Act as a real estate copywriter. Property details: "[beds, baths, sqft,
> location, standout features, price]". Write an MLS-ready listing description:
> a strong opening line, a flowing paragraph on lifestyle and features, a
> bulleted highlights list, and a closing CTA. Warm, accurate, no fair-housing
> violations (no language about buyer demographics).

## 2. Three headline options
> Write 3 attention-grabbing listing headlines (≤80 chars) for this property:
> "[DETAILS]". One emphasizing value, one emphasizing lifestyle, one
> emphasizing location.

## 3. Social caption for a new listing
> Write an Instagram/Facebook caption announcing a new listing: "[DETAILS]".
> Hook in line one, 3 highlights, a clear CTA to DM or book a showing, 5
> relevant hashtags. No fair-housing-sensitive language.

## 4. "Just sold" / "Just listed" post
> Write a short "[Just Listed / Just Sold]" social post for "[AREA]". Build my
> credibility, invite similar sellers/buyers to reach out, keep it humble and
> specific.

---

## Part 2 — Leads & Follow-up

## 5. New-lead first response
> Write a fast, friendly first reply to a new buyer lead who asked about
> "[PROPERTY/AREA]". ≤90 words, answer their question, ask 2 qualifying
> questions (timeline, financing), and offer a next step (call or showing).

## 6. The follow-up sequence
> Write a 4-message follow-up sequence for a lead who went quiet after
> "[CONTEXT]". Space them out (day 2, day 5, day 12, day 25), each short and
> value-first, never pushy, each with one easy CTA.

## 7. Past-client check-in (referrals)
> Write a warm, no-agenda check-in message to a past client I helped buy/sell
> [timeframe] ago. Friendly, personal, gently opens the door to referrals
> without asking outright.

---

## Part 3 — Client Communication

## 8. Explain an offer/counter in plain English
> Explain this offer situation to my client in simple, reassuring terms:
> "[DETAILS]". Cover what it means, their options, and a recommended next
> step framed as their decision. No legal advice.

## 9. Buyer/seller expectation-setting
> Write a clear, friendly message setting expectations for a [buyer/seller]
> about the next 30 days of the process: key steps, what I need from them, and
> typical timelines. Reduce anxiety, sound organized.

## 10. Tough-news message (price drop / no offers)
> Help me deliver tough news to a seller: "[SITUATION, e.g., no offers in 3
> weeks]". Empathetic, data-grounded, ends with a concrete recommendation.
> Two tones: gentle and direct.

---

## Part 4 — Marketing

## 11. Neighborhood guide (lead magnet)
> Write a short, helpful neighborhood guide for "[AREA]": vibe, who it suits,
> nearby amenities, and a line on the market. Useful and shareable, ends
> inviting questions. Avoid steering/fair-housing issues.

## 12. Monthly market-update email
> Write a concise monthly market-update email for my sphere about "[AREA]":
> 3 quick stats (I'll fill in), what it means for buyers and sellers, and a
> soft CTA to reach out. Friendly, credible, ≤200 words.
