10 ChatGPT Prompts to Automate Your Small Business (Free, No Code)

Updated 2026 · ~6 min read

You don't need a developer or a $99/month tool to automate the boring parts of running a small business. With a free ChatGPT or Claude account and the right prompts, you can cut hours of repetitive work every week. Here are practical prompts you can copy and use today — plus how to make them truly hands-off later.

1. Draft lead replies in seconds

Slow replies lose customers. Paste an inbound message and get two ready drafts:

You are my sales assistant. Here is an inbound message from a potential client: "[PASTE]". Write two short, warm reply drafts (max 90 words each): one friendly-casual, one professional. End each with one clear next step.

2. Turn one idea into a week of content

Turn this into a content pack: "[PASTE idea]". Output: 1 LinkedIn post, 3 tweets, 1 Instagram caption with 5 hashtags, and a 45-second video script with a hook in line one.

3. Write invoice and payment reminders

Write a polite payment reminder for an overdue invoice. Client: [NAME], amount [AMOUNT], [X days] late. Give 3 versions: gentle, direct, and final notice.

4. Build a weekly report from rough notes

Here are my notes for the week: "[PASTE]". Produce a 1-page report: Wins, Problems, Numbers (small table), and next week's top 3 priorities.

5. Answer customer FAQs accurately

You are support for [BUSINESS]. Our policies: [PASTE 3-6 facts]. Customer asks: "[PASTE]". Answer in under 120 words, using only those facts. If unsure, ask one clarifying question.

6–10. The rest of the system

The same approach works for cold-email drafts, meeting notes to action items, review requests, social post batches, and spreadsheet cleanup. The key principle: start by copy-pasting manually, prove the prompt saves you time, then wire the winners into a free automation tool (Make.com or n8n) so they run on a trigger.
