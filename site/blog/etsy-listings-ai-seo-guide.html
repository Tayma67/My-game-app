How to Write Etsy Listings That Get Found: An AI Prompt Guide

Updated 2026 · ~6 min read

A beautiful product doesn't sell if buyers can't find it. Etsy is a search engine, and your title, tags, and description decide whether you show up. The good news: you can use a free ChatGPT or Claude account to write listings that match how buyers actually search. Here's the workflow.

Step 1 — Write a keyword-rich title

Front-load the phrases a buyer would type, keep it readable, and use the full character limit:

Act as an Etsy SEO expert. My product: "[DESCRIBE]". Write 5 listing-title options under 140 characters. Front-load the most-searched keywords a buyer would type, stay readable, and avoid words Etsy ignores.

Step 2 — Generate 13 strong tags

Etsy gives you 13 tags. Don't waste them on single words — use buyer phrases:

Give me 13 Etsy tags (max 20 characters each) for: "[DESCRIBE]". Mix broad and long-tail buyer search phrases, include occasion/style/recipient variations, no single-word waste, no repeats of the title.

Step 3 — Write a description that converts

Write an Etsy listing description for "[PRODUCT]": a 2-line benefit hook, bullet specs, who it's perfect for, a shipping/care note, and a soft CTA to favorite or message.

Step 4 — Don't forget buyer messages

Fast, warm communication earns reviews, and reviews boost ranking. Keep templates ready for thank-yous, shipping updates, and the post-delivery review request.

A quick reality check

AI helps you get found and convert — it isn't a traffic magic button. Pair these prompts with consistent listings and good photos, and check Etsy's current policies before publishing. Done steadily, better SEO compounds into more views and more sales.
