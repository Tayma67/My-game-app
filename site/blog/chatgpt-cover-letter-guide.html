How to Write a Cover Letter with ChatGPT (Free Prompts + Examples)

Updated 2026 · ~5 min read

A blank cover letter is one of the most dreaded parts of job hunting. The good news: with a free ChatGPT or Claude account and the right prompt, you can produce a tailored, genuine-sounding cover letter in a few minutes — then edit it to sound like you. Here's exactly how.

Step 1 — Give the AI the right inputs

A good cover letter is specific. Before prompting, gather three things: the job description, your resume or key achievements, and one real reason you want this company.

Step 2 — Use this prompt

Write a cover letter for [ROLE] at [COMPANY]. Use my background: "[PASTE resume or notes]". Here is the job description: "[PASTE]". Rules: 3 short paragraphs, open with why THIS company (reference something specific), middle = 2 relevant proofs, close with a confident call to action. No "To whom it may concern", no clichés.

Step 3 — Fix the "why this company" line

Generic flattery kills a cover letter. Paste the company's About page and ask:

Here's what the company says about itself: "[PASTE]". Write one specific, genuine sentence on why I want to work there — concrete, not flattery.

Step 4 — Make it yours

Read the draft aloud. Cut anything you wouldn't actually say. Add one real detail only you could write. That last 10% is what makes it land — AI gives you the 90% that's just structure and time.

Common mistakes to avoid

Don't let the AI invent achievements; don't send the same letter to every job (the whole point is tailoring); and always match the company's tone — formal for a bank, warmer for a startup.
